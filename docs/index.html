Abstract

Recent Large Language Models (LLMs) have been enhanced with vision capabilities, enabling them to understand images, videos, and interleaved vision-language content. However, the learning methods of these large multimodal models (LMMs) typically treat videos as predetermined clips, making them less effective and efficient at handling streaming video inputs. In this paper, we propose a novel Learning-In-Video-Stream (LIVE) framework, which enables temporally aligned, real-time, and free-form dialogue in a continuous video stream. Our LIVE framework includes comprehensive approaches to achieve video streaming dialogue, encompassing (1) a data generation scheme that converts offline temporal annotations to a streaming dialogue format, (2) a training objective designed to simulate the continuous streaming inputs, (3) an optimized inference pipeline for real-time interactive chat in real-world video stream. With our LIVE framework, we develop a simple VideoLLM-online model and demonstrate its significant advantages in processing streaming video. Meanwhile, VideoLLM-online also showcases state-of-the-art performance on public offline video benchmarks, such as recognition, captioning, forecasting. Code, model, demo will be released as soon as possible.

 

Method

Training method of our LIVE framework. We organize the user-AI dialogue data and video frames in temporal order as the input sequence for an architecture similar to LLaVA~\cite{llava}. To simulate dialogue in streaming video inputs, we employ not only the standard language modeling (LM) loss but also introduce a streaming prediction loss for frame tokens. This additional loss supervises the model when it is necessary to generate language, enabling it to produce temporally aligned responses. This also reduces the redundant dialogue history stored in the key-value cache, leading to more efficient chatting.
